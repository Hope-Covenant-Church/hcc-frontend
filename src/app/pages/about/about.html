ABOUT
HOPE COVENANT CHURCH

Summary

You will notice straight away that family is very important to us. We worship as families holding to the precious promises that God has made to Christians and their households. We believe that Jesus, God's Son, died and rose from the dead to save us and to restore what man originally lost in sinning against the Lord.

That means as individuals we have communion with the triune God, and as households we serve the Lord.

We can sum up what is important to us: it is to live as God's redeemed community in service.

But, What Does That Mean?

We're God's Redeemed Community...

We believe that the church is God's possession and design. God's Son died to bring us back to Himself. God originally created us to love and worship Him and enjoy His presence but when Adam sinned rebellion, disobedience and hate for the Lord entered all our hearts. This is the root of all our troubles and broken lives. The Lord Jesus makes worshipers of us again.

We believe that the Lord Jesus died to save us as individuals and each of us has as a responsibly to seek the Lord's mercy in repentance and faith.

Jesus redeemed His people, dying at Calvary to pay for sin. He removes our guilt and sends the Holy Spirit to cleanse and change our hearts, restoring us to enjoy communion and worship of Him.

Our first great aim then must be to worship God as a multi-cultural group of people according to His commands and design. We are called to be a holy community, the word holy simply means set aside for God. Ephesians 2:14-22.

This community is made up of both individuals and families devoted to the Lord. The scriptures clearly show us that God not only changes our lives but also restores our families. The Bible is filled with instruction to husbands, wives and children. Ephesians 5:15-6:4.

...In Service.

We believe that all Christians are given gifts by the Lord Jesus to serve. Our saviour has called us to be disciples and to obey what he has commanded us as we follow him. This means that each member is able to and called to service in the local congregation.

We also believe that everyone has an obligation to spread the gospel who are without God and without hope. Matthew 28:18-20, Ephesians 2:12-13.

We are also connected to various external mission organizations that we serve with and pray for.

Our Core Values

The Bible and the Gospel

We hold that, the Bible is the only, reliable, sufficient, and non-negotiable norm for learning and living. We are convinced that the Bible is focused on our Lord Jesus Christ and Him crucified. He is the only way we can worship God, with the help of the Holy Spirit.

We believe in the necessity of rebirth, repentance, sanctification, and perseverance. We also believe that gospel preaching is the power of God through which the Holy Spirit enables us to experience these things.

Historical Context

We agree with the historical Reformed Christians' beliefs, as laid out in the five Solas, the doctrines of grace, the regulatory principle for worship, the covenant view of God's plan of salvation, and the relevance of the Old Testament law of God to the New Testament believer. We also acknowledge the Heidelberg Catechism, the Belgic Confession, the Savoy Declaration, the Westminster Confession and the 1689 London Confession to be sound summaries of Christian belief. We are congregational. We place a high premium on the local church as an independent community of true believers.

Our Views on Baptism

Due to the diversity of exegetical views on to the mode and time of baptism, "HCC" allows both infant and believers' baptism. Believers who hold either point of view may become members of the congregation, serve in leadership or on the council and enjoy all the privileges of church membership, provided they qualify on all other grounds required for membership and leadership. Members from different points of view are encouraged to lovingly bear with one another.

One party will not try to coerce the other to comply or do that which their consciences do not allow. This does not exclude robust discussion on the topic to be done in a gracious biblical manner in an attempt for both parties to draw closer to the biblical truth of the matter.

Music Policy

The Selection of Music

We recognise the Biblical requirement to worship God with various songs and hymns during corporate worship (Ephesians 5:19, Psalm 100). We recognise the deep and rich history of ancient hymns and tunes that have been preserved through generations of Christian liturgy but also acknowledge the beautiful scriptural and doctrinal soundness of modern hymn writers.

  • A) They must be theologically sound.
  • B) Hymns must show reverence and awe for God and we should avoid songs that are shallow, flippant, or needlessly repetitive.
  • C) They must reflect our joy in the Lord and in our salvation (Psalm 98:4).
  • D) We all sing together to encourage one another in the Lord. The nature of the songs should be conducive for all to sing.

We encourage our musicians to select music that best helps the congregation of our church to worship God. We recommend that the music we sing during our services takes the congregation from general affirmations, confessions, and worship of God (in the opening few hymns) to more specific and thematic hymns (at the end of the service).
